What is Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)?
Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) is a structured process that formally certifies skills and knowledge you've acquired outside the traditional classroom — whether through informal, non-formal, or on-the-job experience. If you're a craftsperson, an experienced worker, or even unemployed but skilled — and have never had formal qualifications — RPL allows you to earn a nationally recognized certificate that reflects your current competencies.
Why Choose RPL?
Getting certified through RPL will:
  • Validate your skills with an official qualification
  • Improve your chances of employment or promotion
  • Enhance labour mobility — including opportunities abroad
  • Open pathways for further training and education
  • Build your confidence and social inclusion
Who is RPL For?
You may be eligible for RPL if you are:
  • A skilled artisan (e.g. mason, cook, mechanic, hairdresser, electrician, plumber, tailor) without a formal qualification.
  • An employee whose job now requires a certificate due to changing technology or regulations.
  • An unemployed individual with practical skills in a trade who needs certification to access job opportunities.
How does RPL Work? What is the Process?
The RPL journey is simple, guided, and structured. Here’s what it looks like:
  • Awareness Creation & Publicity.
    Learn about RPL and how to apply through outreach programs, social media, and NITA centres.
  • Screening, Guidance & Facilitation.
    Our team will guide you to identify your skills and check if you're ready for assessment.
  • Portfolio of Evidence (PoE).
    You compile documents, photos, testimonials or work samples that demonstrate your experience.
  • Summative Assessment.
    If your PoE qualifies, you undergo a practical and/or written exam to confirm your skill level.
  • Certification.
    Successful candidates receive a nationally recognized certificate from NITA.
What types of certificates can I get through RPL?
You can receive certificates that are equivalent to formal training levels, including craft certificates, technician certificates, and other national trade qualifications recognized by NITA.
How long does the RPL assessment take?
The assessment duration varies depending on your skills and experience but generally takes a few days to a few weeks, including preparation and evaluation.
Can RPL help me get better job opportunities?
Absolutely! Having formal certification increases your credibility, opens up access to bigger contracts, formal employment, and further education or training opportunities.
Is RPL recognized by employers and government agencies?
Yes, certificates awarded through RPL are nationally recognized and valued by employers, government bodies, and professional institutions.
Are there any fees for RPL assessments?
Yes, there is usually a fee to cover the assessment process, but the exact amount varies by institution. Some programs might offer subsidized fees or government support.

What if I do not pass the RPL assessment?
If you do not meet the required standards, you will receive feedback on the skills you need to improve. You can then undergo further training and reapply for assessment.
